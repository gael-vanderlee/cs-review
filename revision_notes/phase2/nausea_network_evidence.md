# Phase 2 — nausea-network evidence check

The original theory section stated that fMRI had identified core networks underlying nausea and sensory integration. Reviewer 1 objected that this framing relied on a small number of fMRI studies and should be related to motion-sickness nausea and vection literature.

## Corpus status

Source: `../review_scripts/data/V4/Stim/Plots_Analysis_GPT54.csv`, checked with pandas on 2026-05-22.

- Eligible fMRI studies: 0.
- Eligible fNIRS studies: 4.
- Eligible EEG studies: 77.
- Eligible neurostimulation-only studies: 11.

## Extra-corpus background references

- `yatesIntegrationVestibularEmetic2014`: Supports vestibular and gastrointestinal signal integration as relevant to nausea and vomiting in motion sickness.
- `cohenNeuralBasisMotion2019`: Supports neural mechanisms of motion sickness involving vestibular inputs, velocity storage, vestibular nuclei, and vestibulo-cerebellar pathways.
- `schmalNeuronalMechanismsTreatment2013`: Supports a clinical/neurophysiological overview of motion sickness mechanisms and treatments. Although the modality subagent did not resolve this item, it was subsequently verified through Zotero item `CJRJCTIN` and DOI `10.1159/000350185`.
- `bertiNeuropsychologicalApproachesVisuallyInduced2020`: Supports neural research on vection, including visual-motion areas and studies using fMRI and EEG.
- `napadowBrainCircuitryUnderlying2013` and `toschiMotionSicknessIncreases2017`: Support extra-corpus fMRI evidence on nausea/motion sickness and sensory integration. These should be framed as background literature, not as included studies in the systematic corpus.

## Corrected manuscript stance

Use extra-corpus fMRI/nausea/vection literature to explain why insular, cingulate, vestibular, cerebellar, and visual-motion systems are relevant to sickness and vection. Then state that the present eligible VR corpus does not include fMRI studies, so this review cannot synthesize fMRI evidence within the included corpus. The manuscript should bridge from that limitation to the EEG/fNIRS/neurostimulation evidence actually reviewed.
