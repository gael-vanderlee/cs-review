# Phase 2 — new and corrected background references

This note records the role of references used for the theory/background rewrite. Citekeys were checked against `references.bib` on 2026-05-22. The Zotero library contains entries for the Phase 2 reference set; `reasonMotionSickness1975` has metadata but no attached PDF.

## Terminology and symptom measurement

- `kennedyResearchVisuallyInduced2010` — Supports VIMS as sickness elicited by visually perceived motion, including cases without physical body motion; DOI `10.1016/j.apergo.2009.11.006`.
- `keshavarzMotionSicknessCurrent2022` — Supports motion sickness as a broader construct with VR and automated vehicles as current application contexts; DOI `10.1097/WCO.0000000000001018`.
- `lacknerMotionSicknessMore2014` — Supports the statement that motion sickness includes symptoms beyond nausea and vomiting and can arise during physical, visual, or virtual motion; DOI `10.1007/s00221-014-4008-8`.
- `chaMotionSicknessDiagnostic` — Supports diagnostic distinction between motion sickness and VIMS by exposure type, and symptom domains including gastrointestinal, thermoregulatory, arousal, dizziness/vertigo, headache/pain, and visual/oculomotor alterations; DOI `10.3233/VES-200005`.
- `PDFMotionSickness` — Lawson HVE chapter; supports motion sickness symptomatology and origins. Use cautiously because the current BibTeX entry is a ResearchGate-style record, but the DOI points to the handbook chapter.
- `cakirHandbookVirtualEnvironments2015` — Keshavarz, Hecht, and Lawson HVE chapter; supports VIMS characteristics, causes, and countermeasures in virtual environments.
- `kennedySimulatorSicknessQuestionnaire1993` — Original SSQ source; use for SSQ nausea, oculomotor, and disorientation subscales.
- `keshavarzValidatingEfficientMethod2011` — Original FMS validation source; use instead of papers that only used the scale.
- `stanneyCybersicknessNotSimulator1997` and `rebenitschReviewCybersicknessApplications2016` — Support the need for caution when comparing cybersickness, simulator sickness, and motion sickness symptom profiles.

## Etiological frameworks

- `reasonMotionSickness1975` — Foundational Reason and Brand book. Zotero has metadata only; use as a foundational citation, but do not base unverified detailed claims solely on it.
- `reasonMotionSicknessAdaptation1978` — Full text verified in Zotero. Supports sensory rearrangement, visual-vestibular/canal-otolith conflict, prior exposure-history, and neural mismatch adaptation.
- `omanMotionSicknessSynthesis1990` — Full text verified through Zotero duplicate with attachment. Supports sensory conflict as a synthesis and explicitly notes that no sensory-conflict neuron/process had been physiologically identified at the time.
- `riccioEcologicalTheoryMotion1991` and `stoffregenPosturalInstabilityPrecedes1998` — Support postural instability as an etiological account distinct from sensory-conflict explanations.
- `hettingerVectionSimulatorSickness1990`, `keshavarzVectionVisuallyInduced2015`, `teixeiraUnexpectedVectionExacerbates2022`, and `teixeiraInvestigatingRelativeContributions2024` — Support vection as visually induced self-motion and as a factor that may contribute to sickness, including the specific unexpected-vection account. Do not label this a generic “vection theory”.
- `treismanMotionSicknessEvolutionary1977` — Supports the toxin-defense/evolutionary account as an explanation for why nausea/vomiting may occur, not as a proximate account of sensory conflict genesis.
- `blesMotionSicknessOnly1998`, `protheroUnifiedApproachPresence2003`, `kramidaResolvingVergenceAccommodationConflict2016`, `ebenholtzMotionSicknessOculomotor1992`, and `nurnbergerMismatchVisualVestibularInformation2021` — Support narrower accounts or mechanisms: subjective vertical mismatch, rest-frame conflict, vergence-accommodation conflict, oculomotor mechanisms, and predictive-coding/mismatch framing.

## Neural and modality background

- `scholkmannReviewContinuousWave2014` and `PresentFutureUse` — Support fNIRS as haemodynamic optical imaging with cortical-depth limitations; avoid claiming temporal resolution superior to fMRI.
- `yeoInvestigatingCorticalActivity2024`, `yamamuraHemodynamicVRAdaptingUsers2021`, `yamamuraPleasantLocomotionReducing2020`, and `tanimuraEffectsLowResolution3D2019` — Active corpus fNIRS references.
- `zhangAnalysisMotionSickness2020` and `gavganiCybersicknessrelatedChangesBrain2018` — Support fNIRS/NIRS use in cybersickness or VIMS studies, but were verified by the source-checking subagent as older/Zotero material rather than active corpus rows.
- `yatesIntegrationVestibularEmetic2014`, `cohenNeuralBasisMotion2019`, and `schmalNeuronalMechanismsTreatment2013` — Support vestibular-emetic and neural background for nausea in motion sickness. `schmalNeuronalMechanismsTreatment2013` was re-verified against Zotero metadata and `references.bib` after the modality subagent flagged it as unresolved.
- `bertiNeuropsychologicalApproachesVisuallyInduced2020` — Supports neural correlates of vection and the presence of fMRI/EEG methods in the extra-corpus vection literature.
- `napadowBrainCircuitryUnderlying2013` and `toschiMotionSicknessIncreases2017` — Extra-corpus fMRI nausea/motion-sickness background; do not present them as eligible corpus studies.
