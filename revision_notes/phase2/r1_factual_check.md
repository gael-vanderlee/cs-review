# Phase 2 — R1 terminology and symptom-profile factual check

Reviewer 1 objected to the draft statement that cybersickness is more strongly associated with vection and disorientation, in contrast to oculomotor or nausea-dominant simulator sickness or motion sickness profiles.

## Checked sources

- `chaMotionSicknessDiagnostic`: The Bárány Society criteria distinguish motion sickness and visually induced motion sickness (VIMS) by the provoking exposure: physical motion for motion sickness and visual motion for VIMS. The diagnostic symptom domains include nausea and/or gastrointestinal disturbance, thermoregulatory disruption, alterations in arousal, dizziness and/or vertigo, headache and/or pain, and visual and/or oculomotor alterations.
- `kennedyResearchVisuallyInduced2010`: VIMS can occur when visually perceived motion produces sickness symptoms, including cases without corresponding physical motion.
- `kennedySimulatorSicknessQuestionnaire1993`: The SSQ organizes symptoms into nausea, oculomotor, and disorientation subscales. Vection is not an SSQ symptom subscale.
- `stanneyCybersicknessNotSimulator1997`: Cybersickness and simulator sickness symptom profiles can differ, but the distinction should be presented as empirical variation rather than as a hierarchy where vection is a symptom.
- `rebenitschReviewCybersicknessApplications2016`: Supports caution when comparing cybersickness and motion sickness symptom profiles across devices, environments, and measures.
- `lacknerMotionSicknessMore2014`: Motion sickness includes symptoms beyond nausea and vomiting, including visually or virtually induced cases.

## Corrected statement for prose

Use: Cybersickness is commonly treated as a VR-related form of VIMS, while simulator sickness and other VIMS terms refer to partly overlapping exposure contexts. These constructs are usually measured through symptom reports that include nausea, oculomotor disturbance, disorientation, dizziness/vertigo, thermoregulatory changes, and related symptoms. Vection is the visually induced illusion of self-motion and can covary with sickness, but it is not itself a symptom category.

Avoid: Claims that cybersickness is defined by vection, that vection is a symptom, or that cybersickness lacks nausea or oculomotor involvement.
